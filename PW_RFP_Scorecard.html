Tabs in this scorecard
Completion Tracker
Phase-by-phase status for each of the five vendors. Pick a status (— / ✓ Yes / ✗ No / ◎ Pending) from each cell for the seven RFP phases. Completion percentages and progress bars roll up automatically.
Written Scoring
Score each vendor against the 51 RFP questions. Critical questions weight 3×, High 2×, Medium 1×. Filter by category. Weighted averages calculate automatically.
Live Scoring
Score the finalist live presentation against six dimensions. Used for the second round only. All dimensions weight equally.
Vendor Comparison
Auto-rolled summary of written grade, live grade, and weighted final grade. Weights are editable (default: written 60%, live 40%). Read-only calculations — inputs are status and recommendation fields only.
Scoring rubric — apply consistently across written and live
5
Exceptional
Best-in-class answer, evidence is specific and verifiable, exceeds expectations.
4
Strong
Clearly exceeds expectations, minor gaps.
3
Meets expectations
Adequate, addresses the question, no major concerns.
2
Below expectations
Partial answer, gaps in evidence, raises concerns.
1
Inadequate
Does not address the question, missing evidence, or contradicts requirements.
—
Blank / unscored
Treated as zero in the weighted average. Leaving blank lowers the vendor score — score every question.
Priority weights
Critical ×3Must-haves. A weak Critical answer is grounds for elimination.
High ×2Important. Material to delivery success.
Medium ×1Useful context, lower decision weight.
Sharing and editing protocol
Shared access
All data saves to shared cloud storage. Changes made by any scorer are visible to all others in real time.
One scorer per vendor column
Avoid simultaneous edits to the same vendor column. Coordinate via Teams or Slack to prevent overwrites (last write wins).
Use Notes columns for caveats
If your score depends on a clarification or assumption, write it in the Notes column rather than discounting silently.
Final grades are consensus
Use Notes to log dissent. The final grade is what the team agrees to in the wash-up call.
